The thesis

The best writing on Wall Street never leaves the floor.

The sharpest writing on the Street sits behind a five-figure terminal, a six-figure research seat, or a seven-figure prime brokerage. Most of it never reaches the people who'd actually use it.

TheStreetBrief takes the parts that move things, the macro reads, the desk chatter, the deal sheet, and condenses them into a Sunday read. No takes from people who've never priced a deal. No headlines pretending to be analysis. Just signal, in the voice of someone who's done the job.

A short history

From a meme account to a morning brief.

The brief didn't start as a brief. It started as a Wall Street meme page. The audience grew. The signal got sharper. The brief was the next step.

2021

The page is born.

@afinancebro starts as a finance-meme account on Instagram. Within a year, it crosses 100K followers, by being funny and right, in that order.

2023

Audience scales past a million.

Cross-platform reach passes a million per month. Analysts, associates, and traders DM screenshots of the page in group chats on every floor of every bank.

2024

The brief launches.

The first issue of TheStreetBrief goes out. Markets at the top. Macro in the middle. The deal sheet at the bottom. Quietly added to inboxes across the Street.

2026

Quietly read across the Street.

Today, the brief lands across the bulge, the boutiques, the funds, and the front offices. Read by the people who'd rather lead the take than chase it.

How we write it

Four rules.
No exceptions.

A brief is a contract with the reader's time. We treat it that way.

01

Sourced, not scraped.

Every issue is reported and written. Sources include desks, research, and primary documents. Not aggregators. Not AI summaries.

02

Sharper than the desk.

If a sentence can be cut, it's cut. If a fact won't move the next meeting, it isn't in the brief.

03

Plain English, no PR.

If a company says it, we say what it means. If a Fed governor says it, we say what's priced. No press-release voice.

04

Free. Always.

The brief is free and will stay free. We make our money from brand partnerships. We vet every single one.
